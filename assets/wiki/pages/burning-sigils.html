The light of the crimson sun burns in the heart of the oubliette, breathing stolen life into a world that was condemned to die in darkness.

The sigils are the arteries through which the fires flow, the living words by whose voices the thought of the crimson architects takes form.
Wrought by the archwardens upon the carapace of sacrificed angels, these runes bear a terrible power. Whatever names or commands they invoke carry the power and authority the sun below.
To learn their names is perilous. To scribe them is to weave a chain of fire by which one shall ever more be bound.

For a name is an anchor to the earth, and no hand may grasp flame without sacrifice
