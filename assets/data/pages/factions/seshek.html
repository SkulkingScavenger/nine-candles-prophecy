Believed by many to have gone extinct long ago, a handful of these ancient creatures yet remain to watch over the Candlerests: beacons of soothing light where the wandering dead may find rest, as may the living who know of their secret locations.

The Seshek are inoffensive for the most part, desiring only to continue their work in peace. But they are not defenseless. Indeed, they are the guardians of an ancient and formidable magic, which is believed to be greater even than that of the wardens, though they have seldom had recourse to it in its full might.

As keepers of memories and friends of the dreamlords, the Seshek possess a profound insight into the souls of other creatures. Such empathy is a curse, for like their lost benefactors, they take on the suffering of others as their own

Appearance

The features of the Gravekeepers are rarely seen, on account of their heavy, cowled robes of office. Beneath these, their features are pale and chitinous. Their faces are eyeless, and somewhat resembling large insects. Individuals are easily identified by prominent ornamental crests.
They are taller than humans, but skeletally slight and the few who remain are quite old and bent with age and care.
Even under their robes, Gravekeepers are easily recognized by their pale lanterns

Society

The Remaining Gravekeepers seldom travel far from their candlerests, where they are needed, and correspondence is not easy on account of their secrecy. News does travel between them, slowly.
